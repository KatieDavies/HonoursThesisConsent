Purpose: This experiment looks at your ability to accurately read the strength of graphically presented correlations. The goal of the work is to determine how accurately people interpret graphically presented correlations based on characteristics of how the data and superficial aspects of the graphs are presented.

Procedure: In this experiment, you will be presented with a series of graphs, one at a time. For each graph, you will be shown a scale from -1 to 1. Your task will be to judge the strength and direction of the correlation depicted in each graph. As you learned in your undergraduate class, a correlation of -1 indicates a perfect negative relationship between two variables, a correlation of 0 indicates no correlation between two variables, and a correlation of +1 indicates a perfect positive correlation between two variables. All of the answers will be collected by the computer.

Risks and benefits: There are typically no risks involved in this experiment, greater than what you might experience in your everyday life. You may take any breaks as needed. You are free to stop your participation at any time and without consequence. You will benefit by gaining experience with scientific research in psychology.
